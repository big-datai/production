# LYRICS

[Verse 1]
Pumpkins on the porch glow gold and bright,
Tiny footsteps on a Halloween night.
Sister with a tiara, sister with a stem,
Joe in his ladybug — and where is Ginger then?

[Pre-Chorus]
We knocked on every door, asked everyone we met,
"Have you seen our doggie? She's just a little pet!"
Through the witch's purple smoke, past the pirate's gold,
Past the friendly skeletons — we were brave, we were bold.

[Chorus]
We found Ginger! We found Ginger!
Hiding by the candy house, safe and sound!
We found Ginger! We found Ginger!
Sweetest little doggie that was ever found!

[Verse 2]
Through the cobweb tunnel arch, past the giant cat,
Joe lifted up his nose and he knew just where she sat.
Behind the pumpkin tower, on a bed of leaves,
A little pup in pumpkin cape — the dog that no one leaves.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mrs. Patel kept her warm with water in a bowl,
Said you're the bravest kids on the block tonight, on the whole.
Eva wrapped her in a hug, Sara petted her tail,
Papa knelt and said you did it — you didn't, didn't fail.

[Chorus]
We found Ginger! We found Ginger!
Hiding by the candy house, safe and sound!
We found Ginger! We found Ginger!
Sweetest little doggie that was ever found!

[Bridge]
Walking home with cookies and a cape and a leash,
Two dogs trot beside us in the autumn-evening peace.
Porch lights twinkle softly, leaves crunch underfoot,
A Halloween that started lost ended in a hug.

[Outro]
Goodnight, little Ginger,
Goodnight, little Joe,
Goodnight, little princess,
Goodnight, tiny pumpkin,
We found you — we love you so.

# GENRE

Children's musical, sing-along family pop, warm acoustic indie-folk for kids. Soft strummed acoustic guitar layered with gentle ukulele picking, light glockenspiel sparkles on the chorus, soft hand-claps on the pre-chorus. Lead vocal is a warm friendly female voice in a clear mid-range, double-tracked on the chorus with a softer harmony a third above. Backup voices on the chorus include sweet child-toned harmonies (girls aged 5-9 sounding tone). Tempo around 96 BPM, in major key (G major), 4/4 time, gentle skipping rhythm. Production aesthetic: Bluey theme song meets Mister Rogers meets Frozen lullaby reprise — extremely tender and family-warm, never sad. Cozy autumn evening atmosphere, soft analog warmth, room-tone reverb. Brief solo middle-eight in glockenspiel with gentle string-pad backing. NO heavy drums, NO synth bass, NO electric guitar distortion. Pure cozy acoustic kid-show vibe. Final chord rings out softly on the lullaby outro.
