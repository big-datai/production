# LYRICS

[Verse 1 — Eva solo, 3yo squeaky voice]
There's a tiny little tooth in the palm of my hand,
A wiggle and a giggle and it pops out — bam!
I'll put it under my pillow with a wish so bright,
The Tooth Fairy's coming for me tonight.

[Pre-Chorus — Sara joins, 6yo voice]
But somewhere in the hallway, a little nose sniffed by,
A puppy on a mission, a twinkle in his eye.

[Chorus — both girls, bright and fun]
Tooth Fairy, Tooth Fairy, where do you fly?
Sparkle through the moonlight, sparkle through the sky.
Tooth Fairy, Tooth Fairy, leave me a coin,
But Joe found the tooth first — and Joe doesn't know!

[Verse 2 — Sara solo with Eva harmony]
Detective in the living room, magnifying glass,
Cushions and lamps and shoes — every place to ask.
Then behind the couch we found a tiny little hill,
A tooth, a key, an earring, and a pancake — still!

[Chorus — both, even brighter]
Tooth Fairy, Tooth Fairy, where do you fly?
Sparkle through the moonlight, sparkle through the sky.
Tooth Fairy, Tooth Fairy, leave me a coin,
We're writing you a letter so you know it was Joe!

[Bridge — both, slower]
Dear Tooth Fairy, please understand,
Joe's a good dog and he doesn't have a plan.
Leave us both a coin if you can,
We promise to forgive him — fairy in the moon-land.

[Final Chorus — both, big harmony]
Tooth Fairy, Tooth Fairy, you came back to me!
Two shiny coins and a note that I can see —
"I understand, I understand, Joe's a good dog."
Forgive and forget and we'll hug it out — woof!

[Outro — soft]
Goodnight little tooth, goodnight little Joe,
The Tooth Fairy's magic — now you know.

# GENRE

Children's musical adventure song. Soft acoustic piano intro, warm acoustic guitar, light glockenspiel sparkles on chorus, tiny tinkly bells (fairy-magic sound). Lead vocal: 3-year-old girl voice (toddler-squeaky, sweet, like Eva), joined by 6-year-old girl voice (clear, slightly nasal, like Sara) on pre-chorus and chorus. Backup: warm mom-voiced soft harmony for fill. Tempo 100 BPM, key of D major, 4/4 time. Production aesthetic: Bluey theme + Disney lullaby + Mister Rogers warmth. Light strings on bridge with shimmering harp gliss. Final chorus has children-choir feel with both girls together. NO drum kit, NO synth bass, NO electric guitar, NO heavy production. Cozy-bedtime-magic atmosphere, soft analog warmth, room-tone reverb. Magical-twinkle ambience throughout. Total length 2:00 (120s) — this is critical for the music block visual loop sync.
