# Magic Bottle Whisper

**Genre:** Soft children's wonder ballad / discovery moment
**Tempo:** ~75 BPM (slow, dreamy)
**Mood:** Gentle awe, magical discovery, little-girl sense of wonder
**Voicing:** One young girl solo voice (Eva). Soft and breathy, whispered verses, gently sung chorus. Light second-girl harmony optional in chorus.
**Length target:** ~60 seconds

---

## Lyrics

**[Intro — soft glockenspiel + airy strings + a single bell-shimmer, 4 bars]**

**[Verse 1 — whispered]**
A little glow in the wet sand by the sea,
A tiny bottle waiting, waiting just for me —
What a strange and shiny thing to find!
A message floating up from somewhere kind...

**[Pre-Chorus]**
Pop the cork and slip the paper out,
Read each tiny letter, read what it's about...
Far away an island, far away a friend,
Asking us to come to where the journeys end.

**[Chorus — sung, gentle]**
Magic bottle, magic bottle, what do you bring?
Whispers from the magic forest on a soft sea-wing.
Magic bottle, magic bottle, what do you say?
"I am stuck and need you — please come find a way."

**[Verse 2 — whispered]**
Bubbles drift like little wishes in the air,
Stars above a friend who needs us over there...
We won't leave a fox forgotten in the foam,
We will build a boat and sail them safely home.

**[Bridge — almost spoken]**
Hold on Ginger...
We're coming...
We're coming soon...

**[Final Chorus — gentle, with optional second-voice harmony]**
Magic bottle, magic bottle, what do you bring?
Whispers from the magic forest on a soft sea-wing.
Magic bottle, magic bottle, what do you say?
"Hold on Ginger — we are on our way."

**[Outro — single soft bottle-glass-clink SFX, glockenspiel fade, soft "Ginger..." whisper trails off]**

---

## Notes for Suno

- This song plays under Eva's slow-mo wonder music video at the magic bottle.
- Glockenspiel + soft airy strings + light piano, maybe one airy synth pad. Layer a single shimmer-bell on the chorus drops.
- Whispered verses with gently sung chorus — the shape is similar to ep10's "Pink Tree Whisper" but with a discovery-of-the-message arc.
- No big drums. Tempo is deliberately slow — dreamy and reverent.
- Final whisper "Ginger..." trailing off should feel like a bedtime promise.
