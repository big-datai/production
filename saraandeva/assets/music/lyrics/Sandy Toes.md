# Sandy Toes

**Genre:** Upbeat children's beach pop / sister duet
**Tempo:** ~125 BPM
**Mood:** Sun-drenched joy, sandcastle pride, sister teamwork
**Voicing:** Two young girl voices (call-and-response). Bright, cheerful. Light ukulele + handclaps + soft surf-percussion.
**Length target:** ~60 seconds

---

## Lyrics

**[Intro — 4 bars ukulele + soft wave-sounds]**

**[Verse 1 — Sara, bouncy]**
Sandy toes, salty knees,
Sun on our backs and a soft sea breeze!
Bucket up, shovel down,
Built ourselves a castle in this sandy town!

**[Pre-Chorus — Eva, gleeful]**
Turret turret turret three,
Just for Mama, just for me!
Add a moat and add a flag,
Stick our shovel like a shovel-tag!

**[Chorus — both, big and bright]**
Sandy toes, salty hair,
Castle in the sand and we don't care!
Sandy toes, salty hair —
We can build it ANYWHERE!
(Clap clap clap clap!)

**[Verse 2 — Sara, mock-serious]**
Mama brought the picnic snacks,
Papa fixing all the cracks!
Eva's pouring water in,
Half the moat and on his chin!

**[Pre-Chorus — Eva, giggling]**
Whoops-a-daisy, oops-a-do,
Did I mean to spill on YOU?
Daddy's wet and Daddy's stuck —
Sandy castle, lots of LUCK!

**[Chorus — both, even bigger]**
Sandy toes, salty hair,
Castle in the sand and we don't care!
Sandy toes, salty hair —
We can build it ANYWHERE!
(Clap clap clap clap!)

**[Bridge — quiet whisper to shout]**
(whisper) ...Phase one done...
(building) ...phase two coming...
(big) BUILD THE BOAT!

**[Final Chorus — both shout, hands up]**
SANDY TOES! SALTY HAIR!
CASTLE IN THE SAND AND WE DON'T CARE!
SANDY TOES! SALTY HAIR!
WE CAN BUILD IT ANYWHERE!
(Wooo!)

**[Outro — wave-sound + soft ukulele fade + a tiny giggle]**

---

## Notes for Suno

- Light ukulele driving the rhythm, soft snare on 2 and 4, cheerful kid handclaps on every chorus.
- Add gentle ocean-wave SFX layered low under the verses.
- No autotune. Cute kid voices that sound 5-7 years old, not adults imitating kids.
- Final chorus should be SHOUTED with hands up — peak energy.
- Pull the energy back slightly in the bridge whisper before the final big push.
