# Burgers in the Car

**Genre:** 1950s sock-hop / doo-wop pastiche / classic rock-and-roll
**Tempo:** ~140 BPM (fast sock-hop swing)
**Mood:** Joyful, retro-celebratory, drive-in carnival
**Voicing:** Two young girl voices (duet). Doo-wop call-and-response. Doo-wop "shoo-bop" backing vocals. Classic 50s feel — think early Beach Boys meets Big Bopper for kids.
**Length target:** ~90 seconds

## Notes for Suno

- This is the closing music video so it gets to be the longest (90s).
- Big classic 50s sock-hop arrangement: walking upright bass, brushed snare on 2 and 4, saxophone, doo-wop "shoo-bop" backing vocals, finger snaps.
- Lots of energy. Feel free to lean into the doo-wop pastiche — saxophone solo in the bridge.
- Lead vocals should sound like cute kids doing their best Lloyd Price impression.
- "Burgers in the car" is the show-stopper hook — repeat it as much as feels right in the chorus.
- No modern drums or autotune. Authentically 50s.
